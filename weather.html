    • Global Warming: A long-term increase in Earth's average temperature due to human activities,
    primarily the emission of greenhouse gases like carbon dioxide.
    Heatwaves: Periods of unusually high temperatures, lasting several days or weeks,
    often posing health risks and stressing ecosystems.
    Cause health issues like heatstroke and dehydration, impact crop yields
    Droughts: Extended periods of deficient rainfall that lead to water scarcity,
    affecting agriculture, water supply, and ecosystems.
    They degrade soil quality, lead to crop failures, and threaten food security.
    Storms and Cyclones: Intense weather systems with strong winds and heavy rains;
    cyclones are particularly violent, rotating storms formed over warm ocean waters.
    Result in loss of lives, destruction of infrastructure, and displacement of communities.
    Heavy Rainfall: Episodes of excessive rain within a short time frame,
    often leading to flooding and disrupting daily life and infrastructure.
    It contaminates water supplies, disrupts daily life, and can spread waterborne diseases

    These changes can impact our environment like Ecosystem Disruption,Loss of Biodiversity,Soil Degradation etc.
